About Los Angeles International Airport – LAX

Los Angeles International Airport (IATA: LAX, ICAO: KLAX, FAA LID: LAX) is the major international airport servicing Los Angeles and the surrounding metropolitan region. It is also known as LAX.

LAX lies in the Los Angeles neighborhood of Westchester, 18 miles (30 kilometers) southwest of Downtown Los Angeles, with Westchester's commercial and residential districts to the north, El Segundo's to the south, and Inglewood's to the east. Los Angeles International Airport (LAX) is the nearest airport to the Westside and the South Bay.

Los Angeles World Airports (LAWA), a division of the Los Angeles city government that also maintains Van Nuys Airport for general aviation, manages the airport. The airport has four parallel runways and covers 3,500 acres (1,400 hectares) of land.

Although LAX is the busiest airport in the Greater Los Angeles Area, the region is served by a number of additional airports, including Hollywood Burbank Airport, John Wayne Airport (Orange County), Long Beach Airport, and Ontario International Airport.

In 2021, LAX served more than 28,779,527 passengers.

About New York Airport / John F. Kennedy International Airport – JFK

John F. Kennedy International Airport (IATA: JFK, ICAO: KJFK, FAA LID: JFK) is an international airport in New York City. With nearly 62.5 million passengers handled in 2019, the airport is the major and busiest international air passenger gateway into North America, the world's 20th busiest airport, the United States' sixth busiest airport, and the busiest airport in the New York airport system. More than ninety airlines operate from the airport, with nonstop or direct flights to destinations in all six continents of the world.

JFK is 16 miles (26 kilometers) southeast of Midtown Manhattan, in the Jamaica area of Queens. There are six passenger terminals and four runways at the airport. JFK serves as a hub for American Airlines and Delta Air Lines, as well as JetBlue's major operating base. Pan Am, TWA, Eastern, National, and Tower Air all used JFK as a hub.

In 2021, JFK served more than 16,630,642 Passengers.
